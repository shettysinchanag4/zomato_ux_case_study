01 — App Overview

About the Platform

Zomato is India's leading food delivery and restaurant discovery platform, operating across 1,000+ cities and 24 countries. Founded in 2008, it connects over 500,000 restaurants with millions of hungry users daily.

Competitive Context

Zomato competes primarily with Swiggy in India's food delivery duopoly. While both platforms offer similar services, Zomato differentiates through its restaurant discovery heritage, Zomato Pro membership, and Blinkit integration for instant grocery delivery.

End-to-End Journey Map

Six sequential stages — from opening the app to post-delivery rating — each with real friction points that affect conversion and retention.

03 — SWOT Analysis

Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities & Threats

A UX-lens SWOT to understand where Zomato leads and where it creates friction.

✓ Strengths
  • 50M+ active users across 1,000+ cities
  • Best-in-class restaurant & cuisine discovery
  • Real-time order tracking with live map view
  • Zomato Pro loyalty rewards programme
  • Strong review ecosystem with photos
! Weaknesses
  • Cluttered home screen causes decision paralysis
  • Inconsistent filter UX across categories
  • Checkout has too many upsell interruptions
  • Slow performance on mid-range Android devices
  • Coupon failures are silent with no feedback
→ Opportunities
  • Group ordering for families and friend groups
  • One-tap reorder flow for habitual users
  • AI-driven personalisation from order history
  • Dark mode and full accessibility improvements
  • Voice search for cooking-while-ordering moments
⚠ Threats
  • Swiggy's cleaner, faster checkout experience
  • Price sensitivity in Tier-2 cities post-COVID
  • Blinkit cannibalising Zomato's screen time
  • User trust issues around surge pricing
  • Hyperlocal dark kitchen competitors
04 — Proposed Redesigns

Three High-Impact UX Improvements

Each recommendation addresses a top-priority pain point with a before/after wireframe sketch and design rationale.

01
Decluttered Home Screen with Progressive Disclosure
2–3 sprints High impact P1 Critical
Problem

The current home screen presents 7+ competing UI zones above the fold — banners, chips, trending cards, subscription offers — causing decision paralysis and slowing the user's path to ordering.

Solution

Adopt progressive disclosure: show only search, location, and top 3 personalised cards above the fold. Move promotions and collections to a scrollable "For You" section below.

02
Streamlined Single-Screen Checkout with Inline Upsells
1 sprint High impact P1 Critical
Problem

The checkout flow currently interrupts users with 3–4 separate screens for tips, charity donations, and add-on items before payment confirmation. Each interrupt adds friction and increases cart abandonment.

Solution

Consolidate checkout into a single scrollable screen. Embed optional upsells as compact inline toggles at the bottom of the bill. Auto-select saved address and last-used payment method.

03
Contextual & Delayed Rating Prompt
<1 sprint Medium impact P2 Important
Problem

The post-delivery rating prompt fires immediately when order status changes to "Delivered". Users who are mid-unboxing or settling in are interrupted, leading to low-quality rushed reviews and one-star rage rates.

Solution

Delay the rating push notification by 20 minutes. Make the in-app prompt a non-blocking inline card in Order History — not a modal. Simplify rating dimensions from 7+ to 3: Food, Packaging, Delivery.

05 — Conclusion

Impact Summary & Design Principles

Zomato is powerful but over-engineered for the average use case. All three proposals can be A/B tested with minimal engineering effort and measured against concrete KPIs.

Improvement Effort Impact Priority
Home Screen Redesign 2–3 sprints Conversion +15–25% P1 Critical
Streamlined Checkout 1 sprint Abandonment −20–30% P1 Critical
Delayed Rating Prompt <1 sprint Review quality +30–40% P2 Important

Design Principles Applied

Progressive Disclosure
Surface only what users need at each stage of the journey. Hide complexity until it's actively requested — not pushed onto the screen by default.
Fitts's Law
Primary CTAs (Search, Place Order) must be the largest, most accessible elements on screen. Smaller targets for optional actions reduce accidental taps.
Jakob's Law
Leverage mental models users bring from competitor apps like Swiggy and Amazon. Familiar patterns reduce learning curve and build immediate trust.
Feedback Timing
System responses — ratings, confirmations, nudges — must align with user readiness and emotional state. A delayed, contextual prompt always outperforms an immediate modal.
Implementation Approach

All three recommendations follow a Lean UX approach: each can be A/B tested with a 50/50 traffic split over a 2-week sprint cycle. Success metrics include time-to-first-tap, checkout completion rate, review submission rate, and average session duration. No structural backend changes are required for improvements 02 and 03.

Research Methods Used

  • Heuristic evaluation against Nielsen's 10 Usability Heuristics
  • Competitor benchmarking: Zomato vs. Swiggy across 12 UX dimensions
  • Secondary user research: App store review analysis (500+ reviews, 2024–2025)
  • Wireframe-based cognitive walkthrough across 3 core user flows
